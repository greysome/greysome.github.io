Characterisations of continuous functions

One of the most basic "dual" statements in topology is that given a continuous function \(f : M \rightarrow N\), preimages of open/closed sets in \(N\) under \(f\) are open/closed in \(M\). Let's call the statement for open sets statement \(T\), and the statement for closed sets statement \(T'\). Supposing we have just proven statement \(T\), we can use duality to immediately conclude statement \(T'\), by setting up a commutative diagram.

Let \(\mathcal{P}(M), \mathcal{O}(M), \mathcal{C}(M)\) denote the power set, the set of open subsets, and the set of closed subsets of the topological space \(M\) respectively. Recall that complements of open sets are closed and vice versa, so the operation of taking complements defines a bijection \(\varphi_M : \mathcal{O}(M) \rightarrow \mathcal{C}(M)\). Also, the operation of taking preimages defines a function \(f^{-1} : \mathcal{P}(N) \rightarrow \mathcal{P}(M)\).

Using this notation, the truth of statement \(T\) implies that the restriction \(f^{-1} : \mathcal{O}(N) \rightarrow \mathcal{O}(M)\) is well-defined. We can then define a function \(g : \mathcal{C}(N) \rightarrow \mathcal{C}(M)\) as the arrow making this diagram commute:

Specifically, \(g = \varphi_N^{-1} f^{-1} \varphi_M\). It can be checked that \(g\) does in fact take preimages, and thus statement \(T'\) immediately follows. Note that we can use the exact same approach to conclude statement \(T\) from statement \(T'\).

Inheritance of open/closed sets in a subspace

The approach of the previous section can be applied to prove other statements. Let \(N\) be a subspace of the topological space \(M\), and let \(f : N \rightarrow M\) be the inclusion function (note here \(N\) is the source, instead of \(M\)). Then taking preimages under \(f\) is equivalent to taking intersections with \(N\).

There is a "dual" statement that \(N\) inherits its opens/closeds from \(M\). As before, let statements \(T,T'\) denote the dual statement but for open and closed sets respectively, and assume we already proved statement \(T\). Note that statement \(T\) essentially says that the restriction \(f^{-1} : \mathcal{O}(M) \rightarrow \mathcal{O}(N)\) is well-defined and surjective.

As above, we can let \(g = \varphi_M^{-1}f^{-1}\varphi_N : \mathcal{C}(M) \rightarrow \mathcal{C}(N)\). Since \(g\) also takes preimages, it is equivalent to the operation of intersection with \(N\). Clearly it is also surjective, which proves statement \(T'\).

Openness/closedness in subspace vs big space

There is yet another statement that if \(N\) is open/closed in \(M\), then \(S \subset N\) is open/closed in \(N\) if and only if it is closed in \(M\). It seems that the previous approach doesn't work in this case (one can draw some Venn diagrams to see why), and I also can't figure out a good formalization of the duality principle here. I'll have to continue figuring this one out.
